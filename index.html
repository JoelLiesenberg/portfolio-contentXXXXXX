Hello all, and welcome to my personal website. My name is Joel Liesenberg, and I am currently a second year dual-degree graduate student at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I am pursuing degrees in both International and Regional Studies, focusing in Japanese Studies, and Information Science, focusing in Digital Curation. I was born and raised here in Michigan, spending a majority of my life up north in East Jordan. After graduating from Central Michigan University in 2018 with a BA in English (focusing in English as a Second Language and Creative Writing), I had the opportunity to live and teach just outside of Tokyo, Japan for two years. And now, at the University of Michigan, I hope to combine my love of the humanities and Japan into a future career working in the digital humanities with Japanese materials.

This website houses several pages. The first is a breakdown of several of my previous projects that I have completed as part of my graduate journey, spanning several classes and topics. The next is a look at some personal Japanese translation projects I've started dabbling with in my free time. And as of now, the final page is a gallery collections of images from my time living in Japan. More will hopefully be added as more projects/trips/translations are undertaken in the future.
